Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

In 2014 the total number of people who lacked healthcare by state and the total number living in poverty by state was positively and significantly correlated (r=.957, n=51, p=.01). Poverty and obesity were also positively and significanlty correlated (r=.986, n=51, p=.01). There was no significant correlation between living in poverty and smoking. In other words, states with higher rates of poverty also had higher rates of people without healthcare and higher rates of obesity but not smoking.

In 2014 there was a very small but significant negative correlation between median age by state and percentage of people without healthcare (r=-.385, n=51, p=.01). The older the mean age of the state population the lower the percentage of people in the state without healthcare. There was no significant correlation found between median age by state and percentage of people with obesity. There was no significant correlation found between median age by state and percentage of people with who smaoke.

In 2014 there was a small but significant negative correlation between median income by state and percentage of people without healthcare (r=-.434, n=51, p=.01). There is a small but significant correlation between median income by state and the percentage of people who are obese (r=-.651, n=51, p=.01). The percentage of people who smoked and the median income by state are significantly negatively correlated (r=-.660, n=51, p=.01). This data demonstrates the higher the median income the smaller the percentage of uninsured, obese, and smokers in a state.
